Advised by Andreas Spitz, EPFL

Team AIMAR

Advised by Anil Deane, UMD

Team AIMAR (Artificially Intelligent Medical Assistant Robot) is an undergraduate research team at the University of Maryland, College Park. Metaphor Detection

Advised by Marine Carpuat, UMD

We reproduced the results of Gao et al.'s "Metaphor Detection in Context" and applied their model to the TOEFL essay dataset. We ran experiments on a few different input features and dataset modifications and presented the results at ACL2020. Our FLP Workshop poster is titled "Evaluating a Bi-LSTM Model for Metaphor Detection in TOEFL Essays".

Github: metaphor-toefl

Enhancing Music Addressability

Advised by Raffaele Viglianti, UMD

The "Enhancing Music Addressability" API is a format-independent schema for selecting portions of a digitial score. During my Digital Humanities Research Assistantship at MITH (Maryland Institute for Technology in the Humanities), I wrote a Python library that enables support for the MusicXML file format.

Github: ema-for-musicxml

Human Gait Recognition

This is my high school senior research project. I obtained treadmill silhouette videos from the OU-ISIR database and worked on extracting features like stride length and foot strikes. Later on, I wrote some simple classification code to identify subjects based on training video data.

Github: human-gait
